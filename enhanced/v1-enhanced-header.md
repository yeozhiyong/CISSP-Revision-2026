## DOMAIN 1: SECURITY AND RISK MANAGEMENT (Q1-80)

### Risk Management & Assessment

**Q1.** A company has an asset valued at $500,000. Analysis shows a potential loss of 30% once every 5 years. What is the Annual Loss Expectancy (ALE)?

A) $30,000
B) $150,000
C) $75,000
D) $50,000

**Answer: A) $30,000**
*Explanation: SLE = $500,000 × 0.30 = $150,000. ARO = 1/5 = 0.2. ALE = $150,000 × 0.2 = $30,000.*

**Q2.** Which risk response strategy involves purchasing insurance?

A) Risk Avoidance
B) Risk Mitigation
C) Risk Transfer
D) Risk Acceptance

**Answer: C) Risk Transfer**
*Explanation: Risk transfer shifts the financial impact to a third party (insurance company).*

**Q3.** The PRIMARY purpose of a risk assessment is to:

A) Eliminate all risks
B) Identify and prioritize risks for treatment
C) Transfer all risks to insurance
D) Document security procedures

**Answer: B) Identify and prioritize risks for treatment**
*Explanation: Risk assessment identifies threats, vulnerabilities, and impacts to prioritize risk treatment efforts.*

**Q4.** Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO) represents:

A) The total cost of a risk event
B) The expected frequency of a risk event per year
C) The percentage of asset value lost
D) The time to recover from an incident

**Answer: B) The expected frequency of a risk event per year**
*Explanation: ARO is how many times a risk event is expected to occur annually.*

**Q5.** A risk that remains after implementing security controls is called:

A) Total Risk
B) Residual Risk
C) Inherent Risk
D) Accepted Risk

**Answer: B) Residual Risk**
*Explanation: Residual risk is the risk remaining after controls have been implemented.*

### Security Governance

**Q6.** Which document provides high-level direction and aligns security with business objectives?

A) Standard
B) Procedure
C) Policy
D) Guideline

**Answer: C) Policy**
*Explanation: Policies are high-level documents that establish security program direction and business alignment.*

**Q7.** The PRIMARY difference between a Standard and a Procedure is:

A) Standards are mandatory, Procedures are optional
B) Standards specify requirements, Procedures describe how to implement them
C) Standards are technical, Procedures are business-focused
D) There is no difference

**Answer: B) Standards specify requirements, Procedures describe how to implement them**
